# Mt. Spite

**Type:** Dungeon (Volcano + Bureau Facility)
**Act:** 2
**Connects:** Ashveil City (south, base) — no through-route (dead end, return same way)
**Theme:** Natural power corrupted by corporate infrastructure

---

## Overview

Mt. Spite is an active volcano north of Ashveil City, and the location of the Bureau's primary Resonance Energy research facility. The mountain itself is dangerous — lava flows, volcanic vents, aggressive fire-type Pokemon. But the real danger is what's inside the Bureau's compound built into the mountain's caldera.

This is the game's major Act 2 dungeon. The player uses Cinder's stolen keycard to infiltrate the facility, where they discover the Resonance Archive — a room full of crystals containing the trapped Resonance signatures of dead Pokemon. The truth about the Bureau's grief-harvesting operation is laid bare.

The dungeon has two halves: the natural mountain ascent (wild encounters, trainer battles) and the Bureau facility (story events, grunt battles, the Archive).

## Atmosphere & Design Notes

- **Color palette — Mountain:** Reds, oranges, blacks. Volcanic rock, lava glow, ash particles in the air. Hot colors. The mountain should feel alive and hostile — the ground is warm, the air shimmers with heat, lava flows are visible but not traversable.
- **Color palette — Facility:** Sterile white, clinical blue-grey, fluorescent lighting. The contrast with the volcanic exterior should be jarring. You step from primal nature into corporate sterility. The facility is CLEAN — impossibly clean for the inside of a volcano. The Bureau spares no expense on their research.
- **Music — Mountain:** Intense, volcanic. Deep drums, low brass, rising tension. The rhythm should evoke eruption — building, building, building. Never resolving.
- **Music — Facility:** Quiet, clinical. Electronic ambient. A low hum (the Resonance Collector). Almost no melody — just atmosphere. The silence is the point. This is where they do the quiet evil.
- **Music — Archive:** The hum intensifies. If you listen carefully, there's something beneath it — not quite a cry, not quite silence. An echo of something that was alive. The music should make the player's skin crawl without being overtly horror. It's SADNESS, not FEAR.
- **The Monitors:** These are the game's primary exposition delivery for the Bureau's operation. Each monitor reveals something specific and horrible. The design should make them feel like mundane office equipment displaying monstrous data — the banality of evil in spreadsheet form.
- **The Archive:** The emotional climax of Act 2. Rows of glowing crystals, each labeled with a species and date. The room should be beautiful and horrifying simultaneously. The crystals are warm. They hum. You can almost hear something. This is where the player understands what "Resonance Energy" really means.
- **Post-game changes:** The facility is abandoned. Equipment left behind. The crystals in the Archive are dark — the Resonance signatures have been released by the Core recalibration. The room is silent for the first time. The mountain's eruption activity is calming.
