# Zenith Plateau

**Type:** City (Major) / Pokemon League
**Act:** 3
**Population:** ~12,000
**Gym:** Zenith Trials (4 Trial Leaders / Elite Four) + Champion Blade
**Motto:** "Where decisions are made." (And where consequences are someone else's problem.)

---

## Overview

Zenith Plateau is Absurdia's capital — a massive natural mesa rising from the ocean, its flat summit carved into a city of white marble and glass. It houses the Pokemon League, the regional government, and the Bureau's central headquarters. Power and bureaucracy converge here. The buildings are grand, the streets are wide, and everything is designed to make you feel small.

The player arrives by ship from Gloomharbor. The League challenge here is structured as the Zenith Trials — a gauntlet of four Trial Leaders who double as the Elite Four, followed by Champion Blade. Each trial reveals a piece of the Bureau's full operation.

After defeating Blade, a hidden passage beneath the League opens to the Resonance Core.

### Districts

**Harbor (south)**
- Where the player arrives from Gloomharbor. A clean, efficient dock — white stone, chrome fixtures. Bureau security scans travelers but lets trainers through.
- A sailor offers return trips to Gloomharbor.

**Central Plaza (center)**
- The city's hub. A wide marble plaza with a fountain depicting ancient Pokemon (the Regulators, though nobody knows that yet).
- Pokemon Center, Poke Mart, two hotels, restaurants, shops.
- A protest area where a small group demonstrates against the Bureau. Their signs: "THE GRID RUNS ON TEARS."
- A veteran trainer warns about Echo's trial.

**Government Quarter (west)**
- Courts, administrative offices, public archives. Accessible but mostly background.
- A Government Clerk NPC explains the League/Bureau overlap.
- The archives contain publicly available (sanitized) records about Resonance Energy.

**Bureau Headquarters (east)**
- A tall, imposing tower of glass and steel. Locked to the player — you can't enter.
- A receptionist outside offers to let you file a complaint. "Average response time: fourteen months."
- Bureau employees go in and out. They look stressed. Post-game, the building is being raided.

**League Complex (north, elevated)**
- Accessed via a grand staircase from the Central Plaza.
- The League building is the highest point on the Plateau — white marble, banners, statues of past Champions.
- Inside: the League receptionist, a healing station, and the path to the four Trial Chambers.

## League Complex — Trial Chambers

Each Trial Leader has their own themed arena, arranged in sequence. The player can heal between trials.

### Trial 1: Tempest's Arena — The Storm Platform

- An open-air platform at the plateau's edge. No walls, no ceiling. Wind howls. Storm clouds gather in grid-like patterns.
- Lightning rods and weather monitoring equipment surround the arena.
- The floor is metal grating — you can see the ocean far below.
- **Atmosphere:** Raw, elemental, exposed. The weather CHANGES during battle — sun, rain, hail, sandstorm — all manipulated by Bureau arrays that Tempest is angry about.

### Trial 2: Fossil's Arena — The Excavation Chamber

- An underground room carved from ancient stone. Fossils of enormous creatures are embedded in the walls — the Regulators.
- Excavation equipment, geological samples, and research notes cover every surface.
- The fossils GLOW with residual Resonance Energy — warmer, more golden than Bureau energy.
- **Atmosphere:** Ancient, sacred, scholarly. This is a place of deep history and deeper violations.

### Trial 3: Echo's Arena — The Silent Room

- A perfectly circular room. No decoration. Soft purple light. Complete silence — the game's ambient sound is turned OFF.
- Echo sits in the center, cross-legged, eyes closed. Their Pokemon hover translucently around them.
- The floor has faint patterns that pulse like a heartbeat.
- **Atmosphere:** Eerie, spiritual, heavy. The silence is aggressive — the player should feel like the air itself is listening. If the player has lost Pokemon, Echo addresses them by name. This is the most emotionally intense trial.

### Trial 4 / Champion's Hall: Blade's Throne Room

- A grand hall at the plateau's highest point. Banners, marble pillars, a throne-like chair at the far end.
- The room screams AUTHORITY — everything about it says "this is where power lives."
- A hidden passage behind the throne leads to the Resonance Core (opened after defeating Blade).
- **Atmosphere:** Imposing, regal, guilty. Blade stands before the throne, not on it. They've never been comfortable here. The room should feel like a courtroom where Blade is the defendant.

## Atmosphere & Design Notes

- **Color palette:** White marble, glass, silver, sky blue. The city should feel elevated, clean, powerful. It's the opposite of Gloomharbor's darkness and Ashveil's grime. But the cleanliness is suspect — this is where the people who let the Bureau operate live. The privilege is visible.
- **Music — City:** Grand, orchestral, but with an undertone of unease. Strings and brass. A march that doesn't quite resolve. The city sounds impressive but not comfortable — like the architecture, it's designed to impress, not welcome.
- **Music — Trials:** Each trial has its own theme. Tempest: electric, stormy, urgent. Fossil: ancient, ponderous, revelatory. Echo: near-silent, with ghostly whispers. Blade: epic, tragic, the weight of twelve years.
- **Sound design:** Wind at the plateau's edge. Fountain water in the plaza. Distant government bustle. The League interior should be echo-y — marble floors, high ceilings, footsteps reverberate.
- **Verticality:** Zenith Plateau should feel HIGH. You arrived by sea and climbed to the sky. The views from the plateau edge should show the ocean, distant Absurdia coastline, clouds BELOW you. The League is the highest point — you're literally at the top of the world. This is intentional: the people at the top saw everything and did nothing.
- **Post-game changes:** Bureau HQ is being raided (officers, trucks, boxes of documents). Protestor is on a bench, relaxed, retired their sign. Government buildings are busier — elections happening. Blade has resigned. The Champion's throne is empty. The League still functions but is under new management (the player, symbolically).
