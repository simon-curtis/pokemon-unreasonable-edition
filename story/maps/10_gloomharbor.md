# Gloomharbor

**Type:** City (Major)
**Act:** 2 (climax)
**Population:** ~4,800 (declining)
**Gym:** Dark-type — Leader Murk
**Motto:** "Everybody's got a secret."

---

## Overview

Gloomharbor is Absurdia's port city — a noir-drenched harbor town shrouded in perpetual fog, lit by yellowed streetlamps, and haunted by secrets. It sits on the western coast of the crescent archipelago, serving as the region's primary shipping hub. The docks handle both legitimate cargo and Bureau shipments marked "SENSITIVE MATERIALS."

The city has a detective's atmosphere: rain-slicked cobblestones, jazz drifting from unseen bars, shadows that seem to watch you. Gym Leader Murk is a private detective who has spent seven years investigating the death of Dr. Lumen — Professor Thorn's former trainer. Murk's gym challenge isn't just battles; it's a noir investigation sequence where the player solves a mystery to earn the right to fight.

This is also where Vice Director Sera confronts the player at the docks — the mid-game moral climax.

### Districts

**Harbor District (center-south)**
- The main docks. Fishing boats, cargo ships, Bureau transport vessels.
- Stacks of crates — some marked "RESONANCE ENERGY CORP — SENSITIVE MATERIALS."
- A ship that takes the player to Zenith Plateau (unlocked after clearing the Sera confrontation).
- NPCs: Dockworkers, sailors, a harbormaster.

**Old Town District (west)**
- The oldest part of the city. Narrow alleys, tall buildings that block light, apartments stacked high.
- A jazz bar where Murk drinks coffee and stares at case files every night.
- Residential homes with NPCs who know things but don't say things.
- A back-alley market (optional items, slightly shady).

**Warehouse District (east)**
- Industrial warehouses near the docks. Some active, some abandoned.
- Murk's Gym is here — disguised as a detective's office.
- An abandoned warehouse has lore items if explored (Dr. Lumen's old files, scattered).

**Commerce Area (south-center)**
- Pokemon Center, Poke Mart, and the Police Station.
- The Police Station is understaffed and underfunded. They handle petty crime; anything Bureau-related is "above their pay grade."

## Key Buildings

**1. Murk's Gym / Detective Office (Warehouse District)**
- Exterior: A nondescript building with a neon sign: "MURK — INVESTIGATIONS." Smaller text: "GYM." The sign flickers.
- Interior: Part detective office, part gym. The lobby has a desk, filing cabinets, a coat rack. Behind the desk: the gym proper — a multi-room investigation sequence.

**2. The Foghorn Bar (Old Town)**
- A dimly lit jazz bar. Murk's regular spot. The bar owner knows everyone's business.
- Atmosphere: Dark wood, brass fixtures, a small stage with a saxophone player. Smoke (ambiguous origin). The kind of place where confessions happen at 2 AM.

**3. Police Station (Commerce Area)**
- Small, cramped. Two officers. They're doing their best.
- One officer mentions that Bureau cases are "forwarded to Bureau internal security" and never come back.
- A bulletin board has missing persons flyers — trainers who disappeared after "grief events."

**4. Abandoned Warehouse (Warehouse District)**
- Optional exploration area. Contains scattered documents from Dr. Lumen's original lab (she worked in Gloomharbor before founding the Corporation).
- Documents reveal early Resonance Energy research — positive bond measurements, sustainable grid projections. Foreshadows the resolution.

**5. Docks / Ship Terminal (Harbor)**
- The player boards a ship here for Zenith Plateau after the Sera confrontation.
- The Sera confrontation happens ON the docks, between the gym and the ship.

## Gym Details

### Shadow Badge Gym

**Type:** Dark
**Location:** Warehouse District — detective's office

**Gym Puzzle: The Investigation**
Unlike other gyms, Murk's challenge is a noir mystery. The player must:

1. **Examine the Case Brief** — Learn about Dr. Lumen's missing research data.
2. **Interview Witness 1 (Sailor)** — Battle to earn testimony. Reveals: Bureau trucks at Lumen's lab the night before her death.
3. **Interview Witness 2 (Scientist)** — Battle to earn testimony. Reveals: Lumen found a sustainable alternative. Bureau wanted faster option.
4. **Interview Witness 3 (Bureau Defector)** — Battle to earn testimony. Reveals: "NEUTRALIZE LUMEN RESEARCH INITIATIVE" order signed by Sera.
5. **Assemble evidence on the board** — Player interacts with an evidence board to connect the three testimonies.
6. **Confront Murk** — The "solution" unlocks the battle.

## Key Event: Sera Confrontation

**Location:** Harbor District docks
**Trigger:** After defeating Murk, player walks toward the ship terminal.

Vice Director Sera is waiting with Bureau agents. She presents the 31% argument — positive Resonance alone covers only 31% of grid demand. Shutting down grief harvesting means hospitals go dark. She battles the player (scripted to be winnable but difficult). Win or lose, she delivers the moral challenge and leaves.

## Atmosphere & Design Notes

- **Color palette:** Dark blues, greys, amber (streetlamp light), black. The fog is the defining visual element — it softens edges, mutes colors, and creates a sense of mystery. Streetlamp light pools create warm amber circles in the blue-grey darkness. The harbor water is dark and reflective.
- **Music:** Jazz noir. Slow saxophone, muted trumpet, upright bass, brush drums. The melody should be melancholic, contemplative, smoky. This is the music of late nights and unanswered questions. Inside the gym, the music shifts to a more investigative tone — minor key piano, ticking percussion.
- **Sound design:** Fog horns (distant, periodic), lapping water, creaking dock wood, jazz from the bar (muffled), footsteps on wet cobblestone, distant ship bells. Rain should be light and intermittent. The city SOUNDS wet.
- **Tileset:** Cobblestone streets, brick buildings, dock wood, cargo crates, street lamps with halos of amber light. The buildings are old, weathered, layered with history. The warehouse district is corrugated metal and concrete. The bar uses dark wood and brass. Everything has moisture on it.
- **Fog:** The defining environmental feature. The fog limits visibility at the edges of the screen. Buildings fade into it. NPCs emerge from it. It should feel like the city is hiding something — because it is. Post-game, the fog lifts (the Bureau had fog machines — this is revealed and is both horrifying and absurd).
- **Noir aesthetic:** Gloomharbor is the game's genre moment — it shifts from Pokemon adventure to detective noir. The gym puzzle, the bar, the investigation, Murk's character, the rain, the shadows. Everything supports this. The player should feel like they've walked into a different genre, which mirrors the story's tone shift.
- **Post-game changes:** Fog lifts. The revelation about fog machines is played for dark humor. The bar is brighter. Docks no longer have "SENSITIVE MATERIALS" crates. Murk has closed the Lumen case. The Police Station is busier — they're processing Bureau files now. The ship to Zenith runs regularly.
