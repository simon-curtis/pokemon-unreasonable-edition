# Route S-1 — "The Shortcut"

**Type:** Route (Dangerous)
**Act:** 2
**Connects:** Prismara Town (east) -> Ashveil City (west)
**Theme:** Dangerous, overgrown, deliberately hostile — the Bureau's gauntlet

---

## Overview

Route S-1 is the shortest path between Prismara Town and Ashveil City, and the most dangerous route in the game up to this point. Wild Pokemon here are 8-10 levels above the expected curve. The grass is dense and largely unavoidable. The terrain is overgrown, narrow, and oppressive. A Bureau NPC warns you at the entrance. Another says "We told you" at the exit.

This is the route where the nuzlocke rules become genuinely punishing. Players who have been comfortable will lose Pokemon here. The Stranded Trainer — an NPC who lost their entire team on this route — is the human cost made visible.

The route exists because the Bureau WANTS trainers to suffer here. The danger is engineered. High-level wild Pokemon were deliberately released to increase emotional distress output.

---

A linear, narrow route — three screens, no branching. The player is funneled forward.

### Terrain Features

**Overgrown Path:** The trail is barely maintained. Roots break through the ground. Branches hang low. The undergrowth is thick. This is NOT a pleasant walk — it's a survival corridor.

**Cliff Walls (Screens 1-2):** Natural rock walls on both sides, creating a canyon effect. The sky is a narrow strip above. It feels enclosed, claustrophobic. The player can't go sideways — only forward or back.

**Dense Grass:** Unlike other routes where grass patches can be navigated around, Route S-1's grass fills the entire path width. You WILL walk through it. Multiple times. The encounter rate is high.

**Cliff Edge (Screen 2):** A narrow section where the trail follows a cliff edge. Below is a deep gorge. This is cosmetic — no fall risk — but it creates tension. The drop is visible and the path is narrow.

**Weathered Sign:** Halfway through. Battered by weather. The text is practical, not funny. The humor from Route N-1's signs is gone. This sign is serious.

## Wild Pokemon

**ALL encounters on Route S-1 are significantly above the expected level curve. This is intentional and story-relevant — the Bureau stocked this route.**

| Pokemon | Level Range | Rarity | Notes |
|---------|-------------|--------|-------|
| Graveler | 22-26 | Common | Self-Destruct risk. The nuzlocke killer. |
| Mightyena | 20-24 | Common | Intimidate + strong attacks. |
| Camerupt | 23-27 | Uncommon | Eruption at high HP is devastating. |
| Skarmory | 24-28 | Uncommon | Tanky. Hard to run from. |
| Absol | 25-28 | Rare | High crit rate. Night Slash. Bad omen Pokemon for a reason. |
| Regional aggressive variant (TBD) | 25-30 | Very Rare | Something the Bureau bred/released |

**Design intent:** Every encounter should feel dangerous. Graveler can Self-Destruct and one-shot frail Pokemon. Mightyena hits hard with Intimidate lowering your Attack. Camerupt's Eruption at full HP is a nuke. Absol crits constantly. The player should be SCARED entering every grass patch.

**Nuzlocke note:** This is one encounter area. The player gets ONE catch here — and it'll be something dangerous. Catching an Absol or Skarmory is a silver lining. Graveler Self-Destructing on your catcher is a nightmare.

## Atmosphere & Design Notes

- **Color palette:** Dark greens, browns, grey rock. Desaturated compared to Mirage Meadows. The sky is overcast, visible only through the canyon gap. Ashveil's volcanic ash starts appearing as faint grey particles near the exit. The transition from Prismara's neon to this muted wilderness should be jarring.
- **Music:** Tense. Minor key. Drum-driven. The melody should feel like a heartbeat — rhythmic, urgent, never comfortable. No chorus, no resolution. Just tension sustained for three screens. This is the first route where the music actively makes you anxious.
- **Sound design:** Wind through the canyon. Rustling grass (louder, more aggressive than other routes). Distant Pokemon cries that sound angry, not ambient. Rocks shifting. The cliff edge section should have wind howling from below.
- **Tileset:** Rocky ground, overgrown vegetation, gnarled trees. The path should look barely used — cracks, roots, debris. Cliff walls are textured with dark stone and moss. The grass tiles should be taller and darker than standard route grass — this is WILD grass, not maintained.
- **Pacing:** Route S-1 is SHORT. Three screens. But the encounter density and level spike make it feel much longer. Every step matters. The brevity is the point — you can SEE the exit but getting there is the challenge.
- **Emotional design:** The Stranded Trainer is the route's emotional anchor. They're not a plot NPC — they're a mirror. "This could be you." Their dialog about the Bureau offering a job alongside grief counseling is one of the darkest moments in the game: the system recruits from its own victims.
- **Post-game changes:** The wild Pokemon levels normalize. The grass is still dense but encounters are level-appropriate. The Stranded Trainer is still there — but by choice now. Building a rest stop for other trainers. Healing, not healed.
