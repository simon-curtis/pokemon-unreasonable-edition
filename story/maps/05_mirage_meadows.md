# Mirage Meadows

**Type:** Route
**Act:** 1
**Connects:** The Underlake (south/cave exit) -> Prismara Town (north)
**Theme:** Artificially beautiful, corporately maintained, subtly wrong

---

## Overview

Mirage Meadows is the most visually stunning route in Act 1 — and the most unsettling if you're paying attention. It's a vast flower field of impossible beauty: every color in full bloom simultaneously, perfect weather, gentle breezes, and wild Pokemon that are docile to the point of sedation. The route is maintained by the Resonance Energy Corporation, with small Resonance Pylons hidden among the flowers.

This is the route where Agent Null first appears, and where the player's internal monologue begins connecting dots: the pylons, the docile Pokemon, the corporate sponsorship.

### Terrain Features

**Flower Fields:** Ankle-deep flowers in every color — roses, tulips, lilies, and species that don't exist. The flowers grow in unnaturally perfect patterns: color gradients, geometric arrangements, no weeds. They sway in a breeze that's always gentle, never gusty. If the player examines them closely (interaction prompt near Gardener), they learn the flowers have no roots.

**Resonance Pylons:** Four pylons scattered through the route, increasing in size. They're slender metal poles with a small crystalline tip that glows blue.
- Pylon 1: Small, almost invisible in the flowers. Easy to miss.
- Pylon 2: Near the Gardener. Medium-sized. The flowers around it grow three times as fast (Gardener's dialog).
- Pylon 3: More visible. Hums slightly. Flowers around it are TOO vibrant — colors oversaturated.
- Pylon 4: The largest. Actively humming. Flowers around it don't move in the wind — they're growing toward it. This one is near Prismara Town, where Bureau infrastructure is thickest.

**Manicured Garden (Screen 2):** The wild flower field transitions into a more formal garden — hedges trimmed into shapes, stone paths, benches with the Resonance Energy Corp logo. This is the "corporate" section. It looks like a park designed by a PR department.

**Overlook Point:** A raised area at the north end of Screen 2 where Prismara Town is visible in the distance — a splash of neon color against the horizon. The contrast between the meadow's natural beauty and the town's artificial brightness is deliberate.

**Key behavior note:** All wild Pokemon here should feel off. They don't attack with the usual aggression. Their battle sprites could have a slightly glazed expression. They're not sick — they're suppressed. The Resonance Pylons are dampening their natural instincts.

**Post-game:** After the pylons shut down, the Pokemon here return to normal behavior. Encounters are the same species but they fight properly. The Beedrill are aggressive again (the Ranger is thrilled about this).

## Atmosphere & Design Notes

- **Color palette:** VIVID. Oversaturated greens, reds, yellows, purples, blues. This route should be the most colorful location in the game. It's beautiful. Uncomfortably beautiful. The kind of beauty that makes you wonder who's paying for it.
- **Music:** A gentle, pastoral melody. Flute and strings. Slow, dreamy, a touch too slow. Like a lullaby for a route. The music should feel sedative — it matches the Pokemon's behavior. Post-game, the music tempo increases slightly and adds a more natural rhythm.
- **Sound design:** Wind through flowers (constant, soft), Resonance Pylon humming (gets louder near each pylon), birdsong (but slightly repetitive, like a recording). The ambient sound should feel manufactured — too clean, too consistent.
- **Tileset:** Flower fields should use bright, varied tiles. The flowers should be dense — you're walking THROUGH them. The corporate garden section uses manicured hedge tiles, stone path tiles, and branded bench objects. The Resonance Pylons are unique objects that glow and pulse.
- **The "off" feeling:** Everything about Mirage Meadows should be slightly uncanny valley. Too pretty. Too calm. Too perfect. The player should feel relaxed and uncomfortable at the same time. The internal monologue helps name the feeling, but the environment should create it first.
- **Pylon progression:** The pylons increase in size and visibility, training the player to notice them. By Pylon 4, the player should be looking AT them, not past them. This visual progression mirrors the story's progression — the Bureau's influence becomes harder to ignore.
- **Post-game changes:** Pylons are deactivated (no glow, no hum). The flowers are messier — some wilting, some growing wild, colors less uniform. It's less pretty and more REAL. The Gardener notes the flowers are growing roots for the first time.
