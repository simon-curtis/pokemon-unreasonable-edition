# Nulltown

**Type:** Starting Town
**Act:** 1 — "The Tutorial That Refuses to End"
**Population:** 7 (sign says so)
**Gym:** None
**Motto:** "We're on the map. Technically."

---

## Overview

Nulltown is the smallest settlement in Absurdia — barely a town, more of an administrative accident. It exists at the southeastern tip of the region's crescent, nestled in a shallow valley between two unremarkable hills. The town has no Pokemon Center, no Poke Mart, no landmarks of any kind. It has three buildings, a dirt road, and an overwhelming sense of irrelevance.

The player moves here at the start of the game because their parent was transferred to the Resonance Energy Corporation's new branch office (which is not in Nulltown — it's two towns over, but housing was cheaper here, because of course it was).

---

## Layout

The town is a single screen — compact, minimal, almost aggressively empty.

### Buildings

**1. Player's House (south-center)**
- A modest two-story home. Moving boxes are still stacked outside.
- **1F:** Living room and kitchen. Mom is here, unpacking. TV in the corner plays Resonance Energy Corp news.
- **2F:** Player's bedroom. PC, bed, desk with a POTION. The room is half-unpacked — posters still rolled up, boxes labeled "STUFF" and "MORE STUFF."

**2. Quin's House (south-east, adjacent to Player's)**
- Nearly identical to the Player's house but with a satellite dish on the roof (Quin's streaming setup).
- **1F:** Quin's parent is here. Friendly, slightly overwhelmed by Quin's content creation obsession.
- **2F:** Quin's room. Recording equipment, ring light, a whiteboard with "CONTENT CALENDAR" written on it. A poster: "EVERY DAY IS CONTENT."

**3. Professor Thorn's Field Lab (north-center)**
- A converted barn. Hay bales repurposed as furniture. Lab equipment sits on workbenches made from old feeding troughs. Bookshelves line the walls — half Pokemon research, half Dr. Lumen's personal library.
- The three starter Pokemon are on a hay bale near the entrance, each in a Poke Ball on a small stand.
- A translation device charger is plugged into the wall (Thorn's speech module).
- A framed photograph on the desk: a woman (Dr. Lumen) standing next to a Dartrix, both smiling. The frame is worn from being held.

### Exterior Features

**Town Sign (entrance, south):**
"NULLTOWN — We're on the map. Technically."

**Dirt Road:**
A single unpaved road runs north-south through town. It connects to Route N-1 at the north exit. There are no other exits — Nulltown is a dead end.

**Mailbox (player's house):**
Overstuffed with Resonance Energy Corp promotional materials. "Welcome to Absurdia! Your feelings power our future!"

**Flower patch (between houses):**
A sad, wilting garden. Three flowers. One is plastic. The gardening here reflects the town's energy.

**Quin's filming spot (front of Quin's house):**
A slightly worn patch of ground where Quin stands to film. There are tripod marks in the dirt.

## Wild Pokemon

None. Nulltown has no tall grass, no water, no encounters. It is deliberately sterile — the first grass is on Route N-1. This reinforces the "nothing happens here" identity.

## Key Events

1. **Opening cutscene:** Player arrives via moving truck. Mom gives exposition.
2. **Meeting Quin:** Player exits house, Quin introduces themselves.
3. **Professor Thorn's Lab:** Starter selection, Pokedex received, nuzlocke rules explained.
4. **Rival Battle 1:** Quin challenges you outside the barn after starter selection.
5. **Post-game:** Updated dialog from Mom, Nulltown Resident, and Thorn. Quin returns here for the final rival battle (Battle 7).

## Atmosphere & Design Notes

- **Color palette:** Muted greens and browns. Desaturated. This town is deliberately underwhelming — it should feel like the least interesting place in the world, which makes leaving it feel exciting and makes returning post-game feel meaningful.
- **Music:** A simple, quiet theme. Acoustic guitar, maybe a single woodwind. Unremarkable on purpose. Post-game, the theme should have a subtle warmth added — same melody, richer arrangement.
- **Size:** One screen. The player should be able to see every building from any position. Nulltown is not a place to explore — it's a place to leave. And then come back to.
- **Tile design:** Dirt paths, wooden fences, basic houses. The barn should look like a barn that someone put lab equipment in, not a lab that looks like a barn. The conversion is half-hearted and charming.
- **Weather:** Clear skies. Always. Nulltown is too boring for weather.
