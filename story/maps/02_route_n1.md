# Route N-1 — "The Obvious Path"

**Type:** Route
**Act:** 1
**Connects:** Nulltown (south) -> Ferro City (north)
**Theme:** Comically over-tutorialized, deceptively normal

---

## Overview

Route N-1 is the first route in the game and the most aggressively "helpful" stretch of road in Absurdia. It's a straight, well-maintained path through rolling hills and light forest — the kind of route that exists in every Pokemon game. What makes it unique is the sheer density of tutorial NPCs, informational signs, and Bureau employees casually taking notes on passing trainers.

The route is deliberately conventional on the surface. Tall grass, a few trainers, item pickups. But the player's internal monologue and the Bureau presence plant the first seeds of unease.

### Terrain Features

**Main Path:** A wide dirt road with signposts. Well-maintained. Almost TOO well-maintained for a route between a nothing town and an industrial city. The Bureau pays for upkeep — it's a high-traffic trainer route, which means high-value data collection.

**Tall Grass Patches:** Three patches, increasing in density. Standard early-game encounters. Patch 1 is thin (easy to navigate around), Patch 2 is moderate, Patch 3 is thick (harder to avoid). This is the player's first encounter with nuzlocke rules — one catch per patch type.

**Wooded Areas:** Light deciduous forest on either side of the path. Trees are healthy, normal-looking. Nothing sinister here... yet. The contrast with later routes (Mirage Meadows' rootless flowers, Route S-1's overgrowth) is intentional.

**Ledge:** A one-way ledge near the north end allows quick backtracking from Ferro City to mid-route. Standard Pokemon route design.

**Hidden Tree:** Tutorial NPC 5 is behind a tree off the main path. You have to actively explore to find them. This rewards curiosity with a comedic payoff.

## Atmosphere & Design Notes

- **Color palette:** Bright, standard Pokemon greens and blues. This route should look and feel like every early Pokemon route ever made. The normalcy is the point — it's the baseline against which later routes feel wrong.
- **Music:** Upbeat, adventurous, classic Pokemon route theme. Slightly too cheerful. The kind of music that loops without you noticing, like elevator music for a journey.
- **Density:** The route is SHORT. Two screens. The comedy comes from how much tutorial content is packed into such a small space. You can't walk 10 tiles without someone trying to teach you something.
- **Bureau presence:** The Bureau employees are positioned casually, blending in. They're not blocking paths or being threatening. They're just... there. Taking notes. Measuring things. The player should feel observed without feeling threatened. Yet.
- **Post-game changes:** Tutorial NPCs get updated dialog. Bureau employees are replaced with former employees dealing with unemployment. Signs unchanged — they're permanent installations.
- **Design philosophy:** Route N-1 teaches the player by being annoying about teaching the player. It's a meta-commentary on tutorial design that also functions as an actual tutorial. The Bureau presence is the first storytelling seed — subtle enough to miss on first pass, obvious in hindsight.
