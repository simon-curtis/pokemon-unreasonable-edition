# Prismara Town

**Type:** Town (Major)
**Act:** 1 (ending) / Act 2 (transition)
**Population:** ~5,000
**Gym:** Fairy-type — Leader Glint
**Motto:** "Live Colorfully!" — A Resonance Energy Community. (Mandatory fun district.)

---

## Overview

Prismara Town is Absurdia's showpiece — a resort town built and funded entirely by the Resonance Energy Corporation as a demonstration of what their power grid can achieve. It's Las Vegas meets Disneyland meets a tech company campus. Neon signs, free amenities, color-coordinated everything, massive screens playing Resonance Energy ads on loop. It is aggressively pleasant.

Beneath the surface, Prismara is a data collection hub. Every trainer who visits generates Resonance Energy data that the Bureau monitors. Glint's gym battles are live-streamed — entertainment and measurement simultaneously. The town is a cage painted to look like a playground.

This is where Act 1 ends with the Marowak Incident — the first on-screen Pokemon death.

### Districts

**Town Square (center)**
The heart of Prismara. A large open plaza with:
- A crystal fountain that pulses with Resonance Energy (blue-white glow)
- A raised stage for public battles and events
- Massive screens on buildings playing Resonance Energy Corp ads
- Benches, food stalls, street performers
- This is where the **Marowak Incident** occurs at the end of Act 1

**Resort District (west)**
- **Prismara Hotel:** Free for trainers. Luxurious. "Compliments of the Resonance Energy Corporation." The rooms have Resonance meters on the nightstands — "for your comfort." Heals Pokemon when you rest.
- **Prismara Spa:** An NPC offers "Resonance Alignment" for your Pokemon — cosmetic only, but the language is weird: "Optimizing your bond for maximum energy output!"
- Lounge areas, poolside seating (cosmetic)

**Entertainment District (east)**
- **Glint's Gym / Broadcast Studio:** The gym doubles as a live-streaming studio. Ring lights visible through the windows. The building has a massive LED screen showing Glint's face and current viewer count.
- **Arcade:** Mini-games (optional). Bureau-branded machines that measure "fun energy."
- **Souvenir Shop:** Sells Prismara merchandise. Overpriced but subsidized.

**Commerce Area (south)**
- **Pokemon Center:** Standard, but the decor is Prismara-branded. Pink and white. Corporate logos everywhere.
- **Poke Mart:** Well-stocked. Lower prices than normal (Bureau subsidy). In exchange, the shopkeeper reports trainer purchases.
- **Boutique:** Cosmetic items for the player character.

## Key Event: The Marowak Incident

**Location:** Town Square stage
**Trigger:** After defeating Glint AND battling Quin (Rival Battle 3)

**Sequence:**
1. Player walks through Town Square
2. A crowd is gathered around a trainer battle on the stage
3. One trainer's Marowak takes a critical hit
4. Marowak falls and doesn't get up
5. Five-second pause. Crowd goes quiet.
6. Bureau Cleanup Crew arrives (two medics in white coats)
7. They call it "temporary energy depletion"
8. They take the Marowak and escort the distraught trainer
9. The crowd disperses. Nobody comments. Life continues.
10. Player stands alone in the square. Internal monologue: "...what just happened?"

This is the turning point of Act 1.

## Atmosphere & Design Notes

- **Color palette:** NEON. Pinks, magentas, electric blues, bright whites. Every surface is lit. The town should feel like a phone screen at full brightness — beautiful and slightly painful. The color should contrast sharply with Ashveil City's grey, which the player reaches next.
- **Music:** Upbeat electronic pop. Catchy, looping, corporate. Like mall music crossed with a streaming intro theme. It should be the kind of song that gets stuck in your head against your will. Post-game, the music is quieter — some neon signs are off, the town is dimmer but more honest.
- **Sound design:** Ambient chatter, electronic jingles from shops, the fountain's water, distant music from the stage. Screens have their own audio layer (Resonance Energy ads). The town should feel BUSY and LOUD — sensory overload.
- **Tileset:** Smooth, polished surfaces. Neon signage. Glass and chrome. The tiles should feel expensive and artificial — nothing organic, nothing old, nothing worn. This town was built yesterday by a corporation. Every tile says so.
- **Contrast with later locations:** Prismara is the "before" to Ashveil's "after." The player should remember how bright and fun this place was when they're standing in ash-covered streets. And then remember the Marowak, and realize the brightness was a distraction.
- **The Incident:** The Marowak scene should be staged carefully. The crowd is positioned so the player has to walk through them. The battle is visible on the stage. The critical hit happens in a brief cutscene animation. The five-second pause after Marowak falls should feel LONG — no music, no sound, just the player character staring. Then the Bureau arrives with clinical efficiency. This is the emotional core of Act 1.
- **Post-game changes:** Half the neon signs are off (power reduction). The fountain still works but dimmer. The hotel charges now (no subsidy). The shopkeeper no longer reports data. Tourists comment on being able to see the stars for the first time. The town is less pretty and more real. Better.
