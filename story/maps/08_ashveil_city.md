# Ashveil City

**Type:** City (Major)
**Act:** 2
**Population:** ~3,200
**Gym:** Fire-type — Leader Cinder
**Motto:** "We're still here." (Ash advisory level: MODERATE. Breathe shallowly.)

---

## Overview

Ashveil City sits in the shadow of Mt. Spite, an active volcano whose increased activity has blanketed the city in a permanent haze of fine volcanic ash. Everything is grey-tinged — the buildings, the streets, the people. Residents wear face masks. The air quality is bad and getting worse. The Bureau's PR machine insists this is "natural volcanic activity." Gym Leader Cinder insists they're lying.

Cinder is the conspiracy theorist who is right about everything. His gym is a converted warehouse plastered with evidence boards. After defeating him, he gives the player a stolen Bureau keycard that grants access to Mt. Spite's research facility.

This is where Quin's tone shift happens (Rival Battle 4) — they've lost their first Pokemon and the toxic positivity is cracking.

### Key Buildings

**1. Cinder's Gym (east, industrial edge)**
- A converted warehouse on the outskirts of town. The exterior is covered in hand-drawn charts, photographs, newspaper clippings, and red string connecting them. A single word spray-painted above the door: "TRUTH."
- **Interior:** The gym puzzle involves navigating volcanic vent corridors while avoiding Bureau surveillance drones (line-of-sight patrol enemies). If spotted, you battle a Bureau Grunt and get sent back.
- **Cinder's Room:** At the back, behind the evidence board. Cinder stands in front of a massive wall of connected evidence — charts of Resonance Energy spikes correlated with trainer loss events, maps of wild Pokemon aggression patterns, photographs of Bureau trucks entering Mt. Spite.

**2. Pokemon Center (center)**
- Standard facility. The nurse wears a face mask. Air purifiers hum in every corner. The healing jingle sounds slightly muffled through the ash.

**3. Poke Mart (center)**
- Sells standard supplies plus face masks and ash-resistant items. Air Filter (new item — prevents Sandstorm damage in overworld? Or cosmetic).

**4. Residential Houses (west)**
- 3-4 houses with ash-covered rooftops. Residents discuss the worsening air quality, Cinder's conspiracy theories, and the Bureau's PR responses.
- One house has a coughing old man who remembers when the ash was seasonal.

**5. Mt. Spite Trailhead (north)**
- The path to Mt. Spite. Blocked by a Bureau checkpoint until the player gets the keycard from Cinder.

**6. Bureau PR Office (center, near Pokemon Center)**
- A small, clean office that stands out against the ash-covered surroundings. A Bureau PR representative distributes subsidized air filters and talks about "community wellbeing."

## Atmosphere & Design Notes

- **Color palette:** GREY. Muted everything. The volcanic ash mutes all colors — buildings are grey, the sky is grey, the ground is grey. Small pops of color exist (Cinder's gym has bright red string on the evidence board, the Bureau PR office is aggressively clean/white). The palette should feel suffocating.
- **Music:** Heavy, somber. Low brass, muffled drums. The melody should feel weighed down — like the air itself. A slight industrial edge (shared DNA with Ferro City's theme but darker). Cinder's gym theme should be more energetic — frenetic, conspiratorial, fast tempo.
- **Sound design:** Wind carrying ash (constant whooshing), muffled voices (through masks), the distant rumble of Mt. Spite. Air purifier hum inside buildings. The outdoor ambience should feel oppressive — you can HEAR the bad air.
- **Tileset:** Ash-covered surfaces. Grey-tinted rooftops, streets, trees. Ash drifts against buildings like snow. The Bureau PR office stands out — clean tiles, bright lighting, no ash (they have industrial air purifiers). Cinder's gym uses industrial/warehouse tiles — metal walls, concrete floor, fluorescent lights.
- **Cinder's evidence board:** This should be a visual centerpiece. Red string, photographs, charts, newspaper clippings, handwritten notes. It should look obsessive and crazy at first glance — and then, when the player learns the truth, it should look CORRECT. The design should reward a second look.
- **Post-game changes:** The ash is thinning. Sky is lighter. Residents are removing masks. Cinder's evidence board now has a plaque: "HE WAS RIGHT." The Bureau PR office is closed. The air is cleaner. Mt. Spite's rumbling is quieter.
